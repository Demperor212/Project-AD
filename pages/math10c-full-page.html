What is Math 10C?

Math 10C is the first mathematics course in high school (in Alberta anyway) and is a prerequisite to Math 20 and Math 20IB.


What's in Math 10C?

Math 10C goes over three (3) main subjects; relations and functions, measurement and trigonometry, and algebra.


Relations and functions

Here are the topics you will usually encounter relating to relations and functions:

Graphing
Graph without the use of a graphical calculator a set of data and determine the domain and range of a graph. Determine slope and/or the equation of a given graph.
Relations vs functions
There is a difference between relations and functions, in wich all functions are relations but not all relations are functions. You will learn to identify which is which.
Linear relations
A direct and constant relationship between two variables, where a change in one variable produces a proportional change in the other, resulting in a straight line when graphed. You wil learn about the different forms of linear equations and the relationship between two linear equations.

Of course, that's not an exhaustive list because there's more specific units within each topic.

Measurement and trigonometry

Here are the topics you will usually encounter relating to measurement and trigonometry:

Imperial vs Metric units
Comparison between the two measurement systems and their conversions, incuding solving problems that require a conversion to be done.
Surface area and volume
Find the surface area and/or volume of certain objects, sometimes with conversions being required.
Trigonometry
A branch of mathematics focused on the relationships between the angles and side lengths of triangles, particularly right-angled triangles in the case of Math 10C. Includes trigonometric functions sine, cosine, and tangent.

Of course, that's not an exhaustive list because there's more specific units within each topic.

Also, it is highly unlikely that you will be unfamiliar with the acronym SOHCAHTOA after this unit.

Measurement and trigonometry

Here are the topics you will usually encounter relating to algebra:

Polynomials
Algebraic expressions containing variables, coefficients, exponents and constants.
Factoring
The next part of polynomials where you break down a polynomial into its factors. Also includes expansions, the reverse of factoring.
Roots and powers
These are inverse operations of each other. An exponent (or power) indicates repeated multiplication of a base number, while a root finds a number that, when multiplied by itself a certain number of times, results in the original number.

Of course, that's not an exhaustive list because there's more specific units within each topic.
