How do I study effectively?

Grade 9 is a big transition from middle school. The workload increases, and the expectations are higher. Here are some study habits that actually work (based on experience):

1. Don't cram.
Cramming might work for a quiz, but it will fail you on unit tests and finals. Your brain needs time to process information. Study a little bit every day, even if it's just 10-20 minutes reviewing notes or doing a few practice questions.

I do know some people that can cram a 100 in unit tests, but that is usually not us. For me, I need to cram + daily review, as my brain capacity is less than 1kb.

2. Active recall > Passive reading.
Re-reading your notes over and over is only effective to an extent. Close your notebook and try to explain the concept (to a gen-ai maybe?). If you can't explain it without looking, you don't know it yet.

3. Practice questions are your best friend.
Especially for math and sciences. Doing textbook problems, past exams, or online quizzes will help you identify gaps in your understanding. When you get a question wrong, don't just move on—figure out WHY you got it wrong.

4. Use your planner (or a digital calendar).
Write down all your assignments, tests, and deadlines. Break big projects into smaller tasks with their own deadlines. This prevents the "I forgot that was due tomorrow" panic. Do you know you can make reminders with Siri? It is very good for a quick idea/ reminder.

5. Know when to ask for help.
Gen-ai exists! They can solve 99% of the questions you have. If you need help in social/english, however, go to tutorial and ask for help from teachers.

6. Sleep matters.
Pulling all-nighters is counterproductive. Your brain consolidates memories during sleep. Getting at least 7 hours of sleep (8 if possible, but I rarely achieve that) will help you retain what you studied and perform better on tests.

Remember: Everyone struggles sometimes. The key is to keep trying different strategies until you find what works for YOU.
